What is the Furcula?

Introduction to the Furcula (Wishbone)

Did you know that some dinosaurs had a special bone called the furcula, also known as the wishbone? It's like a tiny treasure hidden in their bodies! This bone was made by two smaller bones, called clavicles, joining together. But not every dinosaur had this wishbone, just some lucky ones.

Function of the Furcula

Picture this: some dinosaurs, like Velociraptor, Tyrannosaurus rex (the famous T. rex), and Archaeopteryx, had this cool bone called the furcula. Now, imagine this bone was like a superhero's tool belt, helping them move around better. In some dinosaurs, it might have helped with flying or keeping their balance while walking, just like a ninja's balancing stick!

Furcula in Modern Birds

Guess what? Birds today also have this magical bone called the furcula! It's like finding a piece of a puzzle that connects the dots between dinosaurs and birds. Scientists believe that birds might have evolved from dinosaurs that had furculae. So, in a way, this tiny bone helps us understand the link between some dinosaurs, like the fierce T. rex, and the chirping birds we see in our backyards!
