What is the Furcula?

Overview

In some dinosaurs, there was a bone called the furcula, which is also known as the wishbone. It's a special bone formed by the fusion (joining) of two smaller bones called clavicles. Not all dinosaurs had this bone, but some did.

The furcula was found in dinosaurs like Velociraptor, Tyrannosaurus rex, and Archaeopteryx, which is considered one of the earliest bird-like dinosaurs.

Scientists think that the furcula might have helped these dinosaurs move around better. For example, in some dinosaurs, it might have helped with flying or balancing while walking.

Interestingly, birds today also have a furcula, just like some dinosaurs did. This connection suggests that birds might have evolved from dinosaurs that had furculae. So, in a way, the furcula helps us understand the link between some dinosaurs and modern birds!
